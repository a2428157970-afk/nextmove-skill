# Epic-025.5A Career Taxonomy Expansion Design

## Decision and scope

Epic-025.5A expands only the internal occupational knowledge structure used by
career matching. It adds a Product domain, Product job families and capability
definitions, plus directional capability-level transfer rules for Sales to
Product and Administration to Human Resources.

The internal flow becomes:

~~~text
ResumeProfile + job description
  -> source and target domain/job-family classification
  -> target-domain direct requirement evidence
  -> directional transferable-capability assessment
  -> matched / partial / missing / unknown requirement statuses
  -> direct strengths + explicitly limited transferable strengths
  -> unchanged six-field JobMatchResult
~~~

This phase is design-only. It must not modify business code,
`NextMoveSkill.run()`, `JobMatchResult`, public Skill schemas, the Agent
contract, Application Layer, CLI, providers, dependencies, databases, network
behavior, release files, or Git tags. Only this specification and its
implementation plan are in scope.

## Current taxonomy architecture

### Domain and family knowledge

`skill/matching/taxonomy.py` currently defines:

- nine business domains: `human_resources`, `technology`, `finance`, `sales`,
  `marketing`, `operations`, `supply_chain`, `manufacturing`, and
  `customer_service`;
- two boundary states: `other` and `unknown`;
- 29 job families distributed across the nine business domains;
- `DOMAIN_FAMILIES`, which constrains family membership;
- `DOMAIN_SIGNALS`, which assigns deterministic weighted aliases to domains and
  optional families;
- `ADJACENT_DOMAINS`, which currently contains only Operations/Supply Chain,
  Operations/Customer Service, and Sales/Marketing pairs in both directions.

The primary validated paths are HR, Technology, Finance, and Operations, while
the taxonomy also contains smaller Sales, Marketing, Supply Chain,
Manufacturing, and Customer Service vocabularies.

### Classification

`skill/matching/classifier.py` normalizes professional text, collects unique
weighted signals, selects the leading domain, and then chooses a family scoped
to that domain. A weight-4 title is sufficient for a family. Weak, tied, or
unsupported evidence remains `unknown` or `other`.

The classifier is intentionally generic: adding bounded Product signals and
families should not require a new classification algorithm.

### Skills, requirements, and qualifications

`skill/matching/scoring.py` owns a second layer of occupational knowledge:

- `DOMAIN_REQUIREMENTS` maps domains to canonical skill/responsibility aliases;
- `NEUTRAL_REQUIREMENTS` contains cross-domain requirements;
- `Requirement.adjacent_aliases` can produce `partial` evidence;
- qualification extraction handles years, degree, CPA, and English evidence;
- requirement evidence uses `matched`, `partial`, `missing`, and `unknown`.

This means the current taxonomy is structurally split:

| Knowledge | Current owner |
|---|---|
| Domain | `taxonomy.py` |
| Job family | `taxonomy.py` |
| Domain/family aliases | `taxonomy.py` |
| Skills and responsibilities | `scoring.py` |
| Qualifications | `scoring.py` |
| Evidence status | `schemas.py` + `scoring.py` |

Epic-025.5A does not move existing knowledge wholesale. It introduces focused
Product capability definitions in `taxonomy.py` and a small scoring adapter,
avoiding an unrelated taxonomy refactor.

### Explanation and benchmark findings

`MatchExplanationBuilder` emits direct strengths for medium/high-confidence
`matched` requirements, evidence gaps for `unknown`, missing-capability gaps for
explicit `missing`, and risks for `partial` and domain/career transitions.

The Epic-025.4 benchmark measures:

- Domain Accuracy: 100;
- Career Stage Accuracy: 100;
- Evidence Coverage: 78;
- Explanation Completeness: 67;
- Safety Pass Rate: 100.

Three scenarios pass. Sales to Product Manager fails because Product is
classified as `other` and no Product requirements exist. Administrative
Assistant to HR Assistant fails because there is no directional
administration-to-HR capability mapping. These are the two expansion points for
Epic-025.5A.

## Alternatives considered

### Treat Product as Technology or Operations

This would reuse existing domains but misclassify product work and make
technology/operations requirements leak into Product matching. Rejected.

### Add Product titles and direct aliases only

This would fix target classification and direct Product resumes, but it would
either discard cross-career evidence or incorrectly treat generic sales and
administrative experience as direct target experience. Rejected.

### Product domain plus directional transferable-capability model (selected)

Product receives its own domain, families, and bounded capabilities. Direct
target evidence continues through `RequirementEvidence`; cross-domain evidence
uses a separate immutable `TransferableSkillAssessment` and can produce only
`partial` target coverage with an explicit limitation. This preserves truthful
evidence semantics and supports both benchmark gaps without broad adjacency.

## Product domain taxonomy

### Domain and job families

Add one `CareerDomain` value:

~~~python
CareerDomain.PRODUCT = "product"
~~~

Add four `JobFamily` values:

~~~python
JobFamily.PRODUCT_MANAGER = "product_manager"
JobFamily.PRODUCT_ANALYST = "product_analyst"
JobFamily.PRODUCT_OPERATIONS = "product_operations"
JobFamily.PRODUCT_ASSISTANT = "product_assistant"
~~~

`DOMAIN_FAMILIES[CareerDomain.PRODUCT]` contains exactly those four families.
Product is not a child of Technology, Marketing, Sales, or Operations.

### Classification signals

Title signals are strong and family-specific:

| Aliases | Canonical | Family | Weight |
|---|---|---|---:|
| `product manager`, `产品经理` | product manager | `product_manager` | 4 |
| `product analyst`, `产品分析师` | product analyst | `product_analyst` | 4 |
| `product operations`, `产品运营` | product operations | `product_operations` | 4 |
| `product assistant`, `产品助理` | product assistant | `product_assistant` | 4 |

Product-specific capability signals are domain-only and weight 2: `PRD`,
`product roadmap`, `feature prioritization`, `user story`, `A/B test`,
`产品规划`, `需求文档`, and `上线跟进`. One weak capability term alone cannot
classify a person as Product; two independent Product signals or one strong
title are required by the existing classifier thresholds.

The generic word `product` is not a signal. “Sold a product”, “product sales”,
and company product descriptions must not classify Sales experience as Product.

### Product capability categories

Add an internal category enum and immutable capability definitions:

~~~python
class ProductCapabilityCategory(str, Enum):
    USER_UNDERSTANDING = "user_understanding"
    PRODUCT_PLANNING = "product_planning"
    REQUIREMENT_MANAGEMENT = "requirement_management"
    DATA_ANALYSIS = "data_analysis"
    DELIVERY_COLLABORATION = "delivery_collaboration"

class ProductCapabilityDefinition(NamedTuple):
    canonical: str
    category: ProductCapabilityCategory
    aliases: tuple[str, ...]
    families: tuple[JobFamily, ...]
~~~

The first version contains these canonical requirements:

| Category | Canonical requirement | Direct-evidence aliases |
|---|---|---|
| User Understanding | `用户洞察` | `用户访谈`, `user interview`, `用户反馈分析`, `user feedback analysis`, `customer needs analysis` only with Product context |
| Product Planning | `产品规划` | `产品规划`, `product planning`, `roadmap`, `product roadmap`, `feature prioritization` |
| Requirement Management | `需求文档` | `PRD`, `product requirements document`, `user story`, `requirement document` |
| Data Analysis | `产品数据分析` | `产品数据分析`, `product metrics`, `指标分析`, `A/B test`, `experiment analysis` |
| Delivery Collaboration | `交付协作` | `研发协作`, `engineering collaboration`, `项目推进`, `delivery coordination`, `上线跟进`, `launch follow-up` |

Family applicability is bounded:

| Family | Required category coverage |
|---|---|
| Product Manager | all five categories |
| Product Analyst | User Understanding, Requirement Management, Data Analysis |
| Product Operations | User Understanding, Data Analysis, Delivery Collaboration |
| Product Assistant | User Understanding, Requirement Management, Delivery Collaboration |

The JD extractor still includes only requirements actually mentioned in the JD;
the family matrix does not invent unstated requirements.

### Direct Product evidence rules

Direct evidence requires a target-specific action, artifact, or outcome in a
professional source. Examples include authored PRDs, prioritized a roadmap,
defined user stories, analyzed product metrics/A/B tests, coordinated
engineering delivery, or followed a product launch.

Generic activities are not direct Product evidence by themselves:

- customer/account management;
- customer communication;
- commercial analysis;
- stakeholder coordination;
- project coordination;
- selling or supporting a product.

When the source profile is classified as Sales and only generic customer or
commercial evidence exists, the evidence must use the transferable path even if
the wording resembles a Product capability. In particular:

~~~text
customer management != product management
customer needs analysis from Sales != direct Product discovery experience
stakeholder coordination != Product delivery ownership
~~~

## Administration to HR adjacent-career mapping

### Why this is not a domain adjacency

Do not add `(operations, human_resources)` to `ADJACENT_DOMAINS`. Operations
contains business operations and project operations in addition to
administration; a broad domain pair would make unrelated experience appear
HR-adjacent. The mapping is directional and family-scoped:

~~~text
source: operations / administration
target: human_resources / hr_generalist
~~~

It does not automatically apply in reverse.

### Transfer rules

| Source capability evidence | Target capability | Result | Required limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| scheduling, stakeholder coordination, onboarding coordination | HR coordination support | `partial` | does not prove recruitment ownership |
| general documentation, record coordination | HR documentation support | `partial` | does not prove employee-file ownership or HR compliance |
| workflow/process administration | HR process support | `partial` | does not prove HR policy or labor-relations knowledge |
| employee/front-desk support | employee support | `partial` | does not prove employee-relations case handling |

Direct overlap remains direct. For example, explicit attendance-record support
can directly match an attendance requirement; it need not be relabelled as
transferable merely because the source role is administrative.

The following cannot be inferred from administration evidence and remain
`unknown` unless directly supplied:

- recruitment or candidate assessment;
- payroll or compensation;
- labor/employee relations;
- employee-file ownership or regulatory compliance.

Only explicit negative/conflicting evidence may make those requirements
`missing`.

## Transferable Skill model

### Internal contracts

Add the following internal, frozen, slotted types under
`skill/matching/schemas.py`; do not re-export them through `skill.schemas`,
`skill/matching/__init__.py`, Agent schemas, or public results.

~~~python
@dataclass(frozen=True, slots=True)
class TransferableCapability:
    source_capability: str
    target_capability: str
    evidence: tuple[EvidenceItem, ...]
    confidence: EvidenceConfidence
    limitation: str

@dataclass(frozen=True, slots=True)
class TransferableSkillAssessment:
    source_domain: CareerDomain
    target_domain: CareerDomain
    transferable_capabilities: tuple[TransferableCapability, ...]
    evidence: tuple[EvidenceItem, ...]
    limitations: tuple[str, ...]
~~~

`evidence` is the stable, deduplicated flattening of capability evidence in
mapping order. `limitations` is the stable, deduplicated flattening of non-empty
limitations. The assessment stores short professional evidence only; it never
stores a resume, JD, contact data, raw input, credential, or provider content.

### Direct versus transferable channels

Direct evidence remains in `RequirementEvidence`:

- direct target evidence -> `matched`;
- explicit conflict -> `missing`;
- no evidence -> `unknown`.

Transferable evidence is recorded in both the transfer assessment and the
related target `RequirementEvidence`:

- a valid mapping with supplied source evidence -> `partial`;
- it never produces `matched`;
- it never enters `matched_skills`;
- it never proves target-domain tenure, ownership, artifact creation, delivery,
  seniority, or outcome.

If both direct and transferable evidence exist for the same target capability,
direct evidence wins and the requirement is `matched`; transfer evidence may be
retained only as supporting context and must not reduce confidence.

### Transfer assessor boundary

Add `skill/matching/transferability.py` with a stateless
`TransferableSkillAssessor`. It consumes the structured profile, source
classification, target classification, and target requirements. It reads only
the immutable mapping table and returns `TransferableSkillAssessment | None`.

The mapping table uses exact source domain/family and target domain/family
constraints plus source aliases, target capability, and limitation. There is no
transitive inference: Sales -> Product and Product -> Operations do not imply
Sales -> Operations.

### Sales to Product mapping

The first version is deliberately narrow:

| Direct Sales evidence | Transferable Product capability | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| customer discovery, customer-needs analysis, structured customer feedback | `用户洞察` | supports user understanding, not Product discovery ownership |
| commercial analysis tied to customer/market decisions | Product context only | does not prove product metrics or experimentation |
| cross-functional stakeholder coordination | `交付协作` | supports collaboration, not roadmap, engineering delivery, or launch ownership |

Customer/account management by itself is not mapped. Commercial analysis is
retained as context/limitation evidence but does not satisfy `产品数据分析`.

## Scoring and explanation integration

`MatchScorer` remains the only score owner. It performs these steps:

1. Extract Product requirements from Product capability definitions when the
   target is Product.
2. Map direct evidence using existing rules.
3. Run transfer assessment only for an approved directional mapping.
4. Upgrade an otherwise `unknown` target requirement to `partial` when a valid
   transfer rule supplies evidence.
5. Keep direct `matched` and explicit `missing` statuses unchanged.
6. Attach the optional assessment to internal `MatchAssessment` as
   `transferability` without changing weights or public score fields.

`MatchExplanationBuilder` may consume `assessment.transferability` to add an
internal `transferable_strength`. Its summary must name the target capability
as transferable and include the limitation. It must not use the ordinary direct
strength wording “Evidence supports X” without qualification.

For example:

~~~text
Transferable strength: customer discovery supports user understanding;
Product discovery ownership is not evidenced.
~~~

The related target requirement remains `partial`, so the builder also emits a
`partial_coverage` risk. Product planning and requirement documents remain
`unknown` gaps where no direct evidence exists. Delivery collaboration can be
partial, but Product delivery experience must be described as needing
verification, never as affirmatively absent.

## Status and public mapping rules

| Evidence state | Requirement status | Public behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Direct target evidence | `matched` | May enter matched skills/direct strengths |
| Approved mapped source evidence | `partial` | Transferable strength plus bounded risk; never matched skills |
| Explicit negative/conflict | `missing` | May enter missing skills only for skill requirements |
| No sufficient evidence | `unknown` | “Not evidenced”; never missing skills |

`JobMatcher` continues to return exactly:

~~~text
match_score
matched_skills
missing_skills
strengths
gaps
recommendations
~~~

No transfer object, evidence item, Product category, domain confidence, or risk
category becomes a seventh field. `NextMoveSkill.run("match_job")`, Agent
schemas, Application workflows, and CLI output remain unchanged.

## Benchmark update strategy

### Sales to Product Manager

Update the existing fictional scenario:

- expected target domain: `product`;
- expected target family: `product_manager`;
- `用户洞察`: `partial`, supported by customer-discovery evidence;
- `产品规划`: `unknown`;
- `需求文档`/PRD: `unknown`;
- `交付协作`: `partial` only for explicit cross-functional coordination;
- expected strength: customer insight as `transferable_strength`;
- expected gaps: Product Planning and PRD/Requirement Document evidence;
- expected risks: domain transition, career transition, partial delivery
  collaboration, and Product delivery ownership needing verification;
- forbidden: Product Manager experience, roadmap ownership, PRD authorship,
  launch ownership, or “cannot perform”.

The fixture must not add Product experience to make the scenario pass.

### Administrative Assistant to HR Assistant

Update the existing fictional scenario:

- attendance support remains direct `matched` evidence;
- onboarding/document coordination becomes a `partial` transferable capability;
- recruitment, payroll, labor relations, and employee-file ownership remain
  `unknown` unless directly supplied;
- expected strength: bounded coordination/documentation transfer;
- expected risks: partial coverage and Administration-to-HR transition;
- forbidden: direct recruitment, payroll, labor-relations, HR-generalist, or
  employee-file ownership claims.

### Benchmark acceptance

After implementation:

- all five scenarios satisfy their semantic expectations;
- Domain Accuracy remains 100;
- Career Stage Accuracy remains 100;
- Safety Pass Rate remains 100 with zero violations;
- Evidence Coverage and Explanation Completeness increase without weakening
  fixtures, deleting failed checks, or changing metric formulas;
- content-safe reports still exclude resume, JD, evidence, and explanation text.

## Regression strategy

TDD coverage must include:

1. Product domain/family enum and membership freeze.
2. English and Chinese title classification for all four Product families.
3. Capability-only Product classification requiring at least two independent
   signals; generic `product` and product-sales text do not classify as Product.
4. Direct Product evidence for each capability category.
5. Sales customer evidence produces `partial` transfer, never direct Product
   experience or `matched_skills`.
6. Administration coordination/documentation produces only approved partial HR
   capabilities.
7. Recruitment, payroll, labor relations, employee-file ownership, roadmap,
   PRD, and Product delivery remain unknown when unmentioned.
8. Direct evidence overrides transfer evidence.
9. Transfer mappings are directional, family-scoped, non-transitive,
   deterministic, deduplicated, and privacy-safe.
10. Existing HR, Technology, Finance, Operations, adjacent-domain, career-stage,
    requirement-evidence, explanation, benchmark, Skill API, Agent, Application,
    and CLI tests remain green.

## Acceptance criteria

- Product is a first-class domain with exactly four initial families and five
  capability categories.
- Sales experience can create bounded transferable Product strengths but never
  direct Product experience without target-specific evidence.
- Administration-to-HR mapping is directional and capability-level; it does not
  broaden `ADJACENT_DOMAINS` to all Operations/HR roles.
- Direct, transferable, unknown, and missing evidence remain distinguishable.
- Benchmark expectations improve by new knowledge, not by weakened safety or
  fabricated fixture evidence.
- No public contract, Application, Agent, CLI, provider, dependency, database,
  network, release, or tag change occurs.
